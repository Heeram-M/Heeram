India Celebrates 10-Year Anniversary of 2011 Cricket World Cup VictoryAs India commemorates the 10-year anniversary of their historic triumph in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, fans and cricket enthusiasts across the nation are reminiscing about that unforgettable moment when India clinched the prestigious title. Under the leadership of Captain Mahendra Singh Dhoni, India secured a thrilling victory over Sri Lanka in the final held at Mumbai's Wankhede Stadium on April 2, 2011.

  1. Victory in the Final: India won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup by defeating Sri Lanka in the final match held at the Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai on April 2, 2011.
  2. Captaincy of MS Dhoni: Under the captaincy of Mahendra Singh Dhoni, India chased down a target of 275 runs set by Sri Lanka, with Dhoni famously sealing the win with a six in the final over.
  3. Man of the Match: Gautam Gambhir played a crucial role in the final, scoring 97 runs and setting the foundation for India's successful chase. He was awarded the Man of the Match for his outstanding innings.
  4. Yuvraj Singh's Contribution: Yuvraj Singh was instrumental throughout the tournament with both bat and ball, earning the Player of the Tournament award for his all-round performance.
  5. Emotional Victory: The victory was particularly significant for India as it marked their second Cricket World Cup win, having previously won in 1983 under Kapil Dev's captaincy.
